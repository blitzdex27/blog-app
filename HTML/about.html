About the site

A demonstration site


The purpose of this site is to demonstrate my skills in programming and web development to potential employers and practice what I have learnt. I know that there are many rooms for improvement for this site. Some I know, some I don't know. Yes, I really don't know them all since I started programming just recently. As you may have guessed, my skills are just within entry level so I'm only applying for entry level positions.

As you've seen from the home page, this site was built from front-end to back-end. I separated the development of front-end with the back-end through branching with git. I tried to do this on my own way. I know I am nowhere near the best practice or the appropriate method to do this since I haven't got the experience working with a team of developers. So if you are willing to take me to your team, then please go ahead and mail me at blitzdex27@gmail.com.

About me

Dexter Ramos

“A career shifter”

I am currently looking for a company to start my career shift that accepts entry level like me in the programming field. A job such as, but not limited to, "Associate Software Engineer", "Junior Software Engineer", "Full stack Web Developer", and "Programmer/Developer Trainee".

I have just finished a web development course from udemy and am trying to build a demonstration site for employment. As you can see, this is my demo site. This is still in development and I haven't used React here yet. I built this site as my first site to consolidate my knowledge in basics such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, databases, and node. I will upgrade this site eventually using React and other more advanced frameworks out there.

My passion for programming started from playing games when I was a child. At first, we paid someone to install a game on my pc, then I started replicating it. I started buying game CDs and installed them by reading the instructions. When I got into high school, I came across this subject called Visual Basic. We wrote down some code in the computer, and a text box appeared with the text I specified. That shocked my mind, it was a bit similar to boxes that were popping out in my game. I thought to myself, "So, I can create a game that I designed myself."

I studied electrical engineering because of curiosity, and to challenge myself. Although I love programming, I thought that I could just learn it from books, computers, and the internet while studying other majors. I was a child back then and was greedy for knowledge. I was naive, I did not have those resources in my boarding house as the school that offers the least amount of tuition fee is far from home, and I had to rent from shops to do some simple assignments.

So after finishing my degree and passing the board, I was driven by momentum of academic success. I worked as an electrical engineer and as an instructor in a university. However, as time goes by, there was this feeling of emptiness that slowly got bigger. I am doing good with my job but I can't feel a sense of satisfaction and joy. I realized that I set aside my passion for too long, much like I have forgotten about it because I had to give it my all just to pass in my degree and work.

Currently, I am studying programming wholeheartedly. I purchased a full course of Python from Udemy because I can see a good future in this language based on the articles I read. I also purchased a course which teaches in-demand skills in the industry, "Full stack Web Development" that teaches front-end, back-end, and databases taught by Angela Yu. I have completed this course and started building my demo site.

I love reading. If you asked me what I choose between a teacher and a book, I would say both. I know I cannot learn everything in a teacher. Basically, I just try to absorb how the teacher thinks, how he/she does things, his/her method, perspective, and resources. In short, I always thought that a teacher teaches only what is practical because he/she went through it. However, whenever I am listening, there are questions being generated in the back of my mind, so many questions that I lose track of them as the lesson goes by. In my perspective, it is not appropriate to ask deep, specific, and theoretical questions. The teacher prepares the lessons with care, knowing what knowledge you require, and while keeping information that is not yet needed. So if you want a deep, specific, and theoretical knowledge, you need to read books. The teacher's energy was already expended preparing a content delivery method that even a student with a lack of foundation can understand, so I think it is a student's responsibility to read to get more information and build more foundations.

Although I can understand and speak English well, when it comes to expressing myself, it was a bit hard for me. It's like the information is buried very deep inside me.
